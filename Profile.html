I officially started going to the gym when I was in my 1st year in India. The coaches there taught me the basics and some exercises to perform every time I attended the session. I immediately loved challenging myself with heavier weights and rarely skipped a gym session since then. The gym was like a place where I felt productive, a place where I could relieve my academic stress.

Throughout my lifting journey, I went through ups and downs, made several mistakes, but at the end of the day, I had fun, every single step of the way. There were times where the gyms were closed and times where I struggle to balance between my personal life and staying committed to fitness. I have learned to prioritize certain aspects and make compromises to get the best out of my life. I was also once asked, if everything is predetermined according to your genetics (health, disease, muscle gain potential), then why bother trying so hard to achieve something that may not even be possible? I believe that we only know the answer to our genetic potential if we actually do try. Since we only get 1 life/body, why not give our body the exercise it needs and try out best until the end?

I created this blog with the intention of sharing anything that I learnt, including my experiences and opinions. Starting out may be difficult, which is why I hope you know that you are not alone in this journey. We may not be perfect, but we can always be the best version of ourselves.
